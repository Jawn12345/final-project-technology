Steam's Impact on Developers

Steam holds a monopoly over digital distribution. Steam is the leading distributor of digital games for the past 16 years. Steam favors bigger companies, which are typically AAA or AA companies.

“If a game makes more than $10 million, developers get to keep a bigger cut of any extra earnings”

There is a 70/30 revenue split between the developer and Valve. The new Valve policy makes it so that if games make more than $10 million, the split becomes 75/25; if the games make more than $50 million, the split becomes 80/20. The change only benefits bigger companies or developers. In theory, developers are supposed to earn their profits by promoting the game to a bigger audience; however, unpopular games are not favored by the algorithm to begin with.
